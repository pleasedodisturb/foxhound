## Top 5 Detail

### 1. Communication (Influencing)
Finds it easy to put thoughts into words. Good conversationalist and presenter. Turns dry ideas into vivid stories. Hunts for the perfect phrase, dramatic words, powerful combinations. People like to listen.

**Personalized insights:**
- Amuses people with stories, provides pleasant distractions
- Naturally open and honest about strengths and limitations
- Forthright and plainspoken -- people seek his company
- Spontaneously regales people with funny anecdotes, witty comments
- Gift for making people laugh out loud

**Blind spots:** May monopolize discussions. Words alone don't always motivate -- needs to listen too.

### 2. Ideation (Strategic Thinking)
Fascinated by ideas. Finds connections between seemingly disparate phenomena. Delighted by elegantly simple concepts underlying complexity.

**Personalized insights:**
- Finds novel and fresh ways to do things -- people turn to him when struggling with ideas
- Mind-numbing routines bore him; thrives with unexpected requests and changing priorities
- Asks questions, ponders answers, finds underlying causes
- Original and innovative thinker -- bristles at "we've always done it this way"
- Mind is brimming with ideas, eager to share with whoever will listen

**Blind spots:** Too many ideas can overwhelm people. May struggle to follow through -- needs execution partners.

### 3. Strategic (Strategic Thinking)
Creates alternative ways to proceed. Quickly spots relevant patterns and issues in any scenario.

**Personalized insights:**
- Generates innovative ideas with unique perspective
- Identifies goals, devises numerous ways of reaching them, chooses the best
- Sees opportunities, trends, and solutions before peers
- Innovative, inventive, original, resourceful
- Generates ideas quickly, draws clever linkages at a pace some find dizzying

**Blind spots:** Others may misinterpret strategic alternatives as criticism. Thought process may be hard for others to follow.

### 4. Responsibility (Executing)
Takes psychological ownership of commitments. Emotionally bound to follow through. Impeccable ethics create reputation of utter dependability.

**Personalized insights:**
- Consistently works to conclusion -- people have confidence he'll finish
- Volunteers for additional duties, enjoys authority over projects
- Expects to be held accountable for results and words
- Draws on personal accountability when pressed to complete tasks
- Seldom rests until work is done

**Blind spots:** Difficulty refusing requests leads to overcommitment. Too many obligations can crowd out important relationships.

### 5. Activator (Influencing)
Makes things happen by turning thoughts into action. Impatient for action. Believes only action is real.

**Personalized insights:**
- Influences people to produce results and rally around causes
- Interjects technical language that carries authority
- Makes people feel valued by listening and referencing their key thoughts
- Likes making own choices, eager to reach goals by taking charge

**Blind spots:** May charge ahead without a solid plan. Urgency can overwhelm others who need more thinking time.

## Career Implications

The profile screams **visionary leader / strategic advisor / innovation catalyst**. Not an operations manager, not a process enforcer, not a detail tracker.

Best roles leverage the top cluster: communicate a strategic vision (Communication + Strategic + Futuristic), generate creative solutions (Ideation), drive people to act on them (Activator + Command), while adapting to changing conditions (Adaptability) and understanding individuals (Individualization).

The Discipline (#34) and Consistency (#33) at the very bottom explain the Wolt friction perfectly. These are the themes that make someone good at maintaining rigid processes and enforcing uniform standards -- exactly what was expected and exactly what is the antithesis of this profile.
