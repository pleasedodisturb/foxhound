## Interview Prep Level

<!-- CUSTOMIZE: Adjust levels to your background and target roles. -->

Rate how much preparation each role demands relative to your current readiness:

| Level | Label | What it means | Example |
|-------|-------|---------------|---------|
| 1 | Wing it | Direct background match, conversational interview | [Your core role at a similar company] |
| 2 | Light prep | Brush up on 1-2 topics, review specific tools | [Adjacent role, mostly familiar] |
| 3 | Moderate | Study domain-specific material, practice scenarios | [Role requiring some new technical depth] |
| 4 | Heavy | Significant gaps to close, dedicated study needed | [Role in adjacent domain] |
| 5 | New domain | Would need to learn fundamentals from scratch | [Completely different field] |

**Scoring integration:**
- Prep 1-3: no penalty (reasonable investment)
- Prep 4: -0.5 (heavy lift, factor into prioritization)
- Prep 5: -1.0 (unlikely to clear bar without major ramp-up)

**Effort-to-compensation sweet spot:**
The best applications have high fit score + low prep level + good compensation. A 9/10 fit that requires prep level 4 may be worth less of your time than a 7/10 that you can wing.
