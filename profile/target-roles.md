# Target Roles

## The Ideal Role Profile

A position where strategic thinking, communication, AI proficiency, and bias for action are **assets, not liabilities**. Where building systems that automate menial work is celebrated, not feared. Where shipping something real — a product, a pipeline, a tool — matters more than the exact job title.

**The new lens:** Stop optimizing for TPM title matches. Match on *intent* — roles where the work involves building with AI, coordinating complex technical programs, driving product from idea to shipped, or enabling developers and AI practitioners to do their best work.

---

## Tier 1: Apply Now

### Senior TPM at AI-Native Companies
- Companies where AI is the product, not an afterthought. The TPM role means technical architecture coordination, not Google Sheet status tracking.
- **Why it fits:** 10+ years of cross-functional program delivery (Ring, Amazon, CloudMade). Built AI-augmented TPM workflows from scratch. Can manage ML/LLM programs across engineering and science orgs.
- **Where to look:** Databricks, Anthropic, Mistral, Cohere, DeepL, Aleph Alpha, Scale AI, Hugging Face, Lovable.

### Technical Product Manager (AI/ML)
- **Why it fits:** Product sense + technical depth + communication skills. 72% EPP match for Analysis, Planning and Consulting. Built and shipped products spanning hardware, ML, and software.
- **Gap to close:** Formal product management methodology (learnable). Understanding of ML product lifecycle.
- **Where to look:** AI startups, ML platform teams, vertical AI applications, DevSecOps platforms (GitLab, Linear).

### Product Engineer / Staff Product Builder
- **Why it fits:** Sitting at the intersection of product thinking and engineering depth. Roles like Oxide Computer's Product Engineer — where you code, design, document, and ship, often as an early team member. This is a natural home for someone who has always operated in the space between PM and eng.
- **Where to look:** Oxide Computer (Remote, Pacific overlap required), AI-native startups, developer tooling companies, early-stage teams where generalists thrive.

### AI Engineer / AI Tooling Builder
- **Why it fits:** Actively building LLM pipelines, agentic systems, MCP-based tooling. This is a trajectory role — already doing the work, building toward the formal title.
- **Where to look:** AI-native startups, developer platforms, companies building internal AI infrastructure.

### AI Program Lead / Innovation Lead
- **Why it fits:** Strategic (#3), Futuristic (#6), Ideation (#2), Activator (#5). Literally what got me fired at Wolt — but in a place that actually wants it.
- **Where to look:** Companies with genuine AI transformation mandates and executive sponsorship. Series B-D scale-ups building AI teams.

## Tier 2: Strong Fits (Even Without Title Match)

### Solutions Engineer (AI-focused)
- Customer-facing technical role at AI companies. Bridge between product and customer success, often involves building demos, prototypes, integration guides.
- **Why it fits:** Strong communicator, technical depth, builder instinct.
- **Where to look:** Mistral, Anthropic, Cohere, AI infrastructure companies.

### Developer Relations / Developer Advocate (AI)
- For companies where the audience is developers building with AI. Technical writing, talks, community building, tooling, feedback loops into product.
- **Why it fits:** Can code, can communicate, cares deeply about developer experience.
- **Where to look:** Hugging Face, Mistral, AI SDK companies, platform companies.

### Senior Product Manager (AI / Developer Tools)
- **Why it fits:** Deep understanding of developer workflows, built integrated tool systems, strong product instincts.
- **Where to look:** DevSecOps, developer platforms, AI tooling companies.

### Founding / Early-Team Builder
- At the right AI-native company, a founding-team generalist role (whatever the title) that involves building product, driving strategy, and wearing multiple hats.
- **Not a penalty — actively desirable.** Early-stage at a mission-aligned company is often the highest-leverage play.

### Chief of Staff / Head of Operations (Startups)
- **Why it fits:** Strategic + Activator + Command + Communication. Can set direction, communicate it, and drive execution.
- **Where to look:** Series A-C startups that need someone to impose order on chaos.

## Tier 2.5: Consulting / Fractional / B2B Portfolio

### Fractional AI Lead / Fractional CTO
- B2B consulting for 1-3 companies simultaneously. AI transformation strategy, technical program leadership, or product build-outs as an external expert.
- **Why it fits:** 10+ years shipping at scale + AI-native builder = rare combination companies pay 150-200 EUR/hr for. Most companies need this but can't justify a full-time hire.
- **Model A:** Full-time job (covers social insurance) + 1-2 B2B side gigs (with employer approval)
- **Model B:** Pure freelance/consulting with 2-3 clients (higher income, handle own insurance/pension)
- **Where to find:** Toptal, Malt, freelancermap.de, GULP, LinkedIn "consulting", direct network, fractional exec marketplaces

### Contract / Project-Based Roles
- Fixed-term (3-12 month) engagements as AI Program Lead, interim TPM, or product builder. Common in enterprise (SAP, Siemens, automotive).
- **Why it fits:** Same work as full-time but higher daily rate, more autonomy, exposure to multiple domains.
- **Where to look:** Hays, Michael Page, freelancermap.de, GULP, Robert Half Technology

### Consulting Positioning
- **Offer:** "I help companies ship AI products — from strategy to production. I've done it at [big company] scale and startup speed."
- **Ideal clients:** Series A-C startups building AI features, mid-market companies starting AI transformation, enterprises needing external AI program leadership
- **Deliverables:** AI product strategy, technical program setup, LLM pipeline architecture, team hiring/structure, first AI feature shipped

---

## Tier 3: Fallback / Bridge Roles

### Senior TPM (with AI mandate)
- Traditional TPM role but with explicit charter to bring AI tooling to the function.
- **Minimum criteria:** Must have written AI strategy, budget for tools, leadership buy-in.

### Program Manager (Cross-functional)
- Managing complex multi-team programs where strategic thinking and stakeholder management matter more than process templates.

### AI Workflow Engineer / Prompt Engineer (Senior)
- Not dismissing these as junior roles — senior-level positions building production LLM systems, evaluation frameworks, and agentic workflows are genuinely exciting.

---

## Growth Trajectory (Skilling Up — Don't Penalize These Roles)

Actively interested in building toward:
- **AI engineering / ML engineering** (not just PM/TPM) — writing pipelines, not just managing them
- **Full-stack building with AI** — shipping product, not just roadmapping it
- **Technical depth in LLM pipelines, agentic systems, MCP/tool-use** — already building in this space

Roles that offer this trajectory — even if the title or comp is slightly below ceiling — **should not be penalized heavily** if the company, team, and growth path are exceptional. A founding engineer role at Lovable or a builder-track role at Mistral outweighs a Senior TPM at a traditional enterprise.

## Values & Philosophy

Beyond compensation and role fit, strong preference for companies that align with:
- **Privacy & digital rights**: Companies building products that protect users, not exploit them
- **Open source**: Organizations that contribute to the commons, not just extract from it
- **EU sovereignty**: European AI companies and institutions building independent tech capacity
- **Impact over hype**: Mission-driven orgs working on regulation, equality, societal good
- **AI for good**: Companies pushing AI forward responsibly, not just feeding VC cycles
- **Builder culture**: Teams that ship, iterate, and give individuals real ownership
