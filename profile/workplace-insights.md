# Workplace Insights Report

**Assessment Date:** August 19, 2024
**Provider:** Criteria Corp

## Notable Traits

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Cooperative** | Values social harmony, inclined to seek common ground |
| **Extroverted** | Socially outgoing, gregarious, often initiates social interactions |
| **Intellectually Curious** | Creative and unafraid of experimentation, interested in learning and exploration |
| **Self-Confident** | Self-assured and secure |

## Work & Interaction Style

**Cooperative:** Agreeable personality, willingness to work closely with others. Prefers accommodative style over forceful one. Instinctively preserves harmonious relations. Colleagues would describe as friendly and considerate.

**Extroverted:** More extroverted than most. Sociable and outgoing in group settings. Energetic, enthusiastic, active. Prefers roles with frequent socialization. Comfortable talking to a wide variety of people.

## Temperament & Outlook

**Intellectually Curious:** More open to new experiences than average. Embraces exploration and learning. Wide range of interests. Favors novel experiences over routine. Inclined to embrace change, engage in self-reflection. More creative and imaginative than most.

**Self-Confident:** Generally self-assured and confident. Faith in abilities. Not overly prone to self-doubt.

## Strengths

- Neither consistently assertive nor overly deferential -- context-adaptive
- Manageable and coachable, suitable for service-oriented roles
- Sociable and energetic, comfortable initiating interactions, enjoys working with others
- High curiosity and willingness to experiment -- comfortable in evolving roles
- Well positioned to cope with frustrations and setbacks
- High self-confidence, generally free from self-doubt

## Potential Challenges

- Can be too accommodating, especially in leadership roles requiring tough decisions
- Narrowly defined roles with no learning trajectory will feel unsatisfying
- Too much patience can be a liability where urgency is required

## Workplace Comfort Spectrum

| Situation | Comfort Level |
|-----------|--------------|
| Open discussions | Extremely comfortable |
| Change in expectations or job duties | Extremely comfortable |
| Taking the lead in group settings | Extremely comfortable |
| Having clear and well articulated goals | Somewhat comfortable |
| Being exposed to frequent conflict | Somewhat comfortable |
| Having to work alone | Not comfortable (extended periods) |
| Rigid enforcement of rules | Not comfortable |
| A narrowly defined role | Not comfortable |

## Development Suggestions

1. **Interaction Style:** Consider the situation and people to calibrate directness. Learn to go it alone when collaboration reduces efficiency. As an extrovert, recognize that some tasks need solo focus. Make effort to listen more, let others be heard.

2. **Attitudes & Outlook:** Creativity is an asset, but sometimes simple solutions beat complex ones. Ensure solutions can be delivered by others, not just you. Don't let confidence skip preparation -- success often requires planning and effort.

3. **Temperament:** Know when to change course. Tenacity and perseverance are important, but equally important to recognize when an approach no longer adds value.
